# Allamaprabhu Ani

Researcher and mechanical engineer working on computational mechanics, differentiable simulation, and scientific machine learning. I develop transparent, GPU-capable research software for fracture and damage mechanics, and I am interested in applying these methods to engineering problems where simulation, data, and design meet.

I am a PhD researcher at **City, St George's, University of London**, with teaching and research-facing activity at **Queen Mary University of London**.

## Research and software

- **[PhAST](https://cems-lab.github.io/PhAST/)** — a PyTorch-native, GPU-accelerated differentiable physics engine for phase-field fracture. The accompanying [arXiv preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23458) presents the formulation, benchmarks, and inverse analysis.
- **[Machine learning for computational fracture and damage mechanics](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.engfracmech.2025.111778)** — lead-authored review in *Engineering Fracture Mechanics* (2026), written with collaborators at EPFL, the University of Florida, and City St George's.
- **[BJSFM](https://github.com/allamaprabhuani/bjsfm)** — analytical bolted-joint stress-field tooling for anisotropic composite plates.
- **[Geometric Transformations](https://github.com/allamaprabhuani/geometric-transformations)** — a MATLAB Live Script for two- and three-dimensional affine transformations.

## Background

Before doctoral research, I founded Aeroknacks and built aerospace structural-analysis automation tools. The work translated established engineering references, including E. F. Bruhn, Michael Niu, Boeing Design Manuals, and ESDU data, into practical Excel-VBA and engineering-software workflows.

I hold an MTech in Design and Manufacturing from the **National Institute of Technology Silchar** and a B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from **Visvesvaraya Technological University**.
